Google Developer Groups (GDGs) are local communities of developers who are passionate
about Google's technologies and APIs, offering a platform for learning, collaboration, and networking
through events and activities.

Here's a more detailed description:
What they are:

GDGs are volunteer-led local groups of developers who are interested in Google's developer technologies and APIs.
focus:

They focus on providing a space for developers to learn, share knowledge,and collaborate,
covering everything from Android, Chrome, Drive, and Google Cloud platforms
to product APIs like Cast API, Maps API, and YouTube API. Activities:
GDGs host a variety of technical activities,
including meetups, workshops, hackathons, and tech talks. Who can join:
GDGs are open to developers of all skill levels,
from beginners to experienced professionals. Joining a GDG provides opportunities to:
Learn new skills and technologies. Network with other developers.

Collaborate on projects. Stay up-to-date with the latest
trends in the tech industry. GDG on Campus:
GDGs also have a "GDG on Campus" initiative, which focuses
on providing learning opportunities
for aspiring developers in universities and colleges.
Google's Role:
Google supports and recognizes GDG chapters, but doesn't own or manage them.
